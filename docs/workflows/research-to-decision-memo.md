# Research to Decision Memo

## Who It Is For

Analysts, consultants, founders, and managers who need to turn scattered research into a memo that supports an actual decision.

## Work Problem

Research findings pile up without a clear recommendation, forcing the reader to do the synthesis and source-checking work themselves.

## Inputs

- Raw research notes, articles, or excerpts.
- Source links for each note when available.
- The specific decision the memo needs to support.
- Constraints such as budget, timing, stakeholder needs, or risk tolerance.

## Copy-Ready Codex Instruction

```text
Using the research notes below, write a decision memo.

Decision to support:
[state the decision clearly]

Constraints:
[list constraints]

Structure:
1. Summary recommendation in one paragraph.
2. Key findings, each with its source noted.
3. Options considered.
4. Risks or open questions.
5. What would change the recommendation.

Rules:
- Use only claims that appear in the notes below.
- Attach a source to each factual claim.
- If a claim has no clear source, mark it "unsourced - verify" instead of stating it as fact.
- Separate facts, assumptions, and recommendations.

Research notes:
[paste notes with sources here]
```

## Expected Output

A one-to-two-page memo with a clear recommendation, source-tagged findings, explicit uncertainty, and a short list of unresolved questions.

## Human Review Checklist

- Every factual claim has a traceable source or is flagged for verification.
- The recommendation follows from the stated findings.
- The stated constraints are actually respected.
- Competitor, client, employer, or internal names were not added unless present and allowed.
- The memo does not hide uncertainty inside confident prose.

## Failure Modes

- Codex may state plausible facts without a source.
- Codex may give a confident recommendation while ignoring a constraint.
- Codex may over-weight the most recent or most detailed source.

## Source Boundary

Based on OpenAI's public Codex everyday-work framing for research-to-decision memos. The source-tagging and verification requirements here are added review practices.
