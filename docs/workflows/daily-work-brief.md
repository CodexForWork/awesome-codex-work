# Daily Work Brief

## Who It Is For

Operators, managers, consultants, and anyone who needs a same-day summary of priorities, open items, and decisions needed before the end of the day.

## Work Problem

Scattered updates across email, chat, calendar events, and notes make it hard to see what matters today and what is waiting on you versus someone else.

## Inputs

- Today's calendar or task list.
- Relevant emails or messages copied in as plain text.
- Open decisions or blockers you already know about.
- Any privacy rules for what must not appear in the output.

## Copy-Ready Codex Instruction

```text
Using the inputs below, write a daily work brief.

Goal:
Help me start the day knowing what matters most.

Structure:
1. Top 3 priorities today, with why each matters.
2. Items waiting on me, with what is needed to unblock them.
3. Items waiting on others, with who and what if the source states it.
4. Anything time-sensitive or at risk of slipping.
5. Open questions I should resolve today.

Constraints:
- Keep it under one page.
- Do not invent tasks, names, or deadlines.
- If something is unclear, mark it "unclear" instead of guessing.
- Do not include private details in the shareable version.

Inputs:
[paste calendar, messages, and task list here]
```

## Expected Output

A short, scannable brief organized by priority, with sourced items only and no invented tasks or people.

## Human Review Checklist

- Every listed priority traces back to an actual input.
- No names, deadlines, numbers, or commitments were invented.
- Sensitive details are removed before sharing.
- The "top 3" reflects your judgment, not just source order.
- Open questions are clear enough to act on.

## Failure Modes

- Codex may over-prioritize whatever appears first.
- Codex may smooth over ambiguity by guessing at deadlines.
- The output may be too polished for a rough internal brief and hide uncertainty.
