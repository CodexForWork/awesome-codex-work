# Review-Ready Deck Outline

## Who It Is For

Consultants, managers, analysts, and founders who need a first-draft slide structure before building the actual deck.

## Work Problem

Starting from blank slides often means the story and evidence are built ad hoc, slide by slide, instead of planned up front.

## Inputs

- Deck purpose and audience.
- Key points, data, or arguments to include.
- Required structure, template, time limit, or stakeholder constraint.
- Source links for claims or figures.

## Copy-Ready Codex Instruction

```text
Using the details below, write a slide-by-slide outline only. Do not invent slide content or design the deck.

Purpose:
[state the goal of the deck]

Audience:
[who will see it]

Structure:
1. Slide number and title.
2. Key message for that slide in one sentence.
3. Supporting points or data to include, only from what I provide.
4. Suggested visual type, such as chart, table, text, or image.
5. Evidence or source needed.

Rules:
- Do not add statistics, claims, or examples that are not in the details below.
- If a section needs more input before it can be outlined properly, say so.
- Keep one main message per slide.

Deck details:
[paste purpose, audience, key points, sources, and constraints here]
```

## Expected Output

A slide-by-slide skeleton with one clear message per slide, ready for a human to fill in and refine.

## Human Review Checklist

- No statistic, quote, or example appears unless provided.
- Each slide's key message matches the real argument.
- Visual suggestions fit the available data.
- Evidence gaps are flagged before design begins.
- The outline fits the audience and time limit.

## Failure Modes

- Codex may fill thin sections with generic business language.
- Codex may suggest charts that do not fit the data.
- Codex may make the story smoother by hiding missing evidence.

## Source Boundary

Based on OpenAI's public Codex everyday-work framing for slide decks. The outline-only and no-invented-content constraints are added review boundaries.
